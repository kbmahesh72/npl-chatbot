## 1. General Information & Venue

Arrival time: Playing teams must arrive at the ground at least 30 minutes before their scheduled match start, so that if the previous game finishes early, the next can begin immediately and keep the day's schedule on track.

Team leadership responsibility: All captains, vice-captains, scorers, and umpires are strongly encouraged to read this document closely. It is the responsibility of team leadership to ensure every team member is aware of all tournament rules.

Parking: All cars must be parked in the gravel area at all grounds. Parking on the grass is not permitted.

Liability: NPL is not liable for damage to property or persons.

Executive discretion: The NPL Executive Team reserves the right to devise approaches to resolve issues and unforeseen circumstances, and to deal with them at its discretion.

General cricket rules: All other regular ICC cricket rules apply unless specifically superseded by this document.

## 2. Player Safety, Uniforms & Equipment

### 2.1 Safety & Liability

Every participating team must ensure necessary safety precautions are taken for player safety (e.g., protective equipment, medical kit). By participating in NPL Blast 2026, all players acknowledge the inherent risks (falls, injuries, etc.), voluntarily assume full responsibility for their own safety, and release NPL and its Executive Team from any liability arising from participation.

### 2.2 Footwear & Uniforms

Metal cleats / spiked player shoes are NOT allowed.

Every team must wear solid-colored uniforms. No player may play in shorts — there is no exception to this rule. Teams are required to be in similar (matching) uniforms.

### 2.3 Ball & Clothing Colour

A heavy tennis ball with white tape will be used (standard tennis-ball cricket ball). Because of this, white clothing is NOT allowed.

### 2.4 Bat Regulations

Fiber bats are not allowed — only wooden bats are permitted, and every bat must pass through the official gauge.

Maximum length: 38 in

Maximum width: 4.25 in

Maximum edge: 1.56 in

Maximum depth: 2.64 in

## 3. Team Registration, Rosters & Player Eligibility

### 3.1 Squad & Registration

Squad size: Each team must have a minimum of 11 and a maximum of 25 players in the squad.

Registration fee: $425 per team. Non-refundable once the schedule has been released.

Team lock-in: Once the tournament begins, a registered player cannot switch teams.

### 3.2 Profile Pictures

All players must upload a profile picture to cricclubs.com/npl.

Profile pictures must clearly show the player's face (a photo showing only the back of a player with a jersey number is not acceptable). The opposing captain has the right to deny a player at the start of the game if this requirement isn't met.

Captains are responsible for ensuring every one of their players has a compliant, clear-face profile picture.

### 3.3 Roster Management

Minimum to start: At least 8 players from each team are required to start a game.

Weekly roster changes: During the league phase, captains may edit their 25-player roster on cricclubs.com/npl on or before Friday of each week. Rosters lock automatically every Friday at 11:59 p.m.

Ineligible players: Players not listed on the current roster before the Friday deadline are ineligible to play that weekend's league games.

Game-day additions: Adding a new player on game day due to sudden availability changes requires prior league approval — no player may be added once the game has started without that approval.

Knockout lock: Once the knockout stage begins, rosters are locked and cannot be changed.

Playoff eligibility: To play in the playoffs (Eliminator, QF, SF, Final), a player must have played at least one league game.

### 3.4 Ineligible / Misrepresented Players

If a non-registered player is fielded, or a player plays under another player's name/photo, the opposing team or officiating umpire must report the offense the same day or the very next day, with evidence — later reports will not be actioned.

If the offending team wins: the result is awarded to the opposing team.

If the offending team loses: they forfeit 1 point in the standings.

During knockout matches: the win is automatically granted to the opposing team.

## 4. Forfeits, Walkovers & Attendance

Minimum players at toss: By toss time (15 minutes before the scheduled game time), a minimum of 8 players from both teams must be present. A team with fewer than 8 players loses the toss.

Walkover: Declared if a team is not prepared to start on schedule. The walked-over team gets 0 points; the opponent gets 2 points. If neither team is ready, both teams get 0 points.

No-shows: Teams not showing up for league or playoff games with at least 8 players forfeit the game; the opposing team is awarded 2 points.

Mutual forfeit: If both teams forfeit by not showing up, no points are awarded to either team.

Forfeit fine: A $100 penalty applies for every forfeited game in the season. Teams must clear any pending forfeit dues from past seasons before registering for a new season.

Forfeit scoring: A game forfeited at any point (league or playoff) still counts as a played game. The forfeiting team loses; CricClubs calculates run rate from the final registered scores, and the NPL Executive Team will not adjust it regardless of when the game was stopped.

Forfeited-game eligibility: A forfeited league game counts as an eligible game for the non-forfeiting team, subject to review and approval by the NPL Executive Team.

## 5. Scoring & Statistics

The CricClubs scoring app must be used for all official scoring.

All statistics are maintained on the CricClubs site.

Scorers must sit together and record all details required by the NPL Executive Team — runs and wickets per batsman/bowler, catches, run-outs, stampings, etc. — clearly attributed to individual players for stats and awards, and must publish scores to CricClubs at the end of the game.

Bonus points: There is no bonus point system in NPL Blast 2026 (per the CricClubs bonus-point policy, none applies this season).

## 6. Umpiring

Main umpire: A neutral umpire (not from either playing team) officiates league games; assignment is handled by the NPL Executive Team when the schedule is set.

Backup umpires: The NPL Organizing team will try to find neutral umpires for every game. If unavailable, the teams assigned as backup umpires must send one. Teams that fail to send an assigned umpire lose 1 point from the standings or pay a $50 fine, whichever applies.

Leg umpire: The batting team provides the leg umpire for their innings. The leg umpire gives feedback to the main umpire only when specifically asked.

Leg umpire conduct: Coaching or discussing tactics with the striker/non-striker draws a first official warning from the main umpire; a second offense results in a 1-point deduction from the team's standings.

Illegal action: If the leg umpire suspects an illegal bowling action, they inform the main umpire, who determines the course of action.

Fair delivery: The ball must be bowled, not thrown. If the main umpire is not fully satisfied a delivery was fair, they call and signal "no ball" immediately.

Umpire's decision is FINAL. Only the captain and/or vice-captain may raise concerns, and must do so politely. Repeated protests or misbehavior lead to a team warning, and continued issues may result in the NPL Executive Team disqualifying the team from the tournament.

## 7. Match Format, Overs & Fielding Restrictions

Standard format: 20 overs per innings, with a maximum of 4 overs per bowler.

Powerplay: The first 6 overs of each innings are powerplay, with only 2 fielders allowed outside the inner (30-yard) circle. After that, up to 5 fielders may be outside the circle. Exceeding the fielder limit after powerplay is a No Ball.

If a team takes the field with fewer than eleven players, the reduction in fielders shall apply only inside the 30-yard circle. The maximum number of fielders permitted outside the circle shall remain unchanged.

During Powerplay (Overs 1–6):

Maximum 2 fielders outside the circle.

After Powerplay (Overs 7–20):

Maximum 5 fielders outside the circle regardless of the number of players available.

There is no restriction on the number of fielders on the leg side.

## 8. Batting Rules

Free hit: A no-ball results in a free hit for the batting team.

No LBW (leg before wicket) dismissals.

No leg byes: runs off a leg bye are not credited, and there are no run-outs if a batsman attempts a run off a leg bye.

Byes are allowed.

Overthrows: runs from overthrows are credited to the batsman on strike for the ball bowled before the overthrow, and are NOT recorded as extras.

Caught dismissals: the new batter always takes strike, regardless of whether the batters crossed before the catch was taken — unless it is the end of an over.

Retired Not Out: a batsman who is injured, ill, or has another unavoidable reason to leave the field may retire and resume their innings at the next dismissal or retirement.

Retired Out: a batsman is retired out if they retire without the umpire's permission and without the opposing captain's permission to resume.

Wide balls: all deliveries passing down the leg side are called Wide, except when the ball goes over the stumps.

## 9. Bowling & No-Ball Rules

Breaking non-striker's stumps in the delivery stride is a No Ball.

A full toss above waist height is a No Ball, even for a spinner (to avoid confusion).

One bouncer per over is allowed.

Beamers: the first beamer in a game earns the bowler a warning; a second beamer in the same game suspends that bowler from bowling for the rest of the game.

A delivery that bounces more than once before reaching the batsman is a No Ball (not a dead ball).

A ball that pitches outside the turf pitch is a No Ball.

A batsman cannot be stumped off a No Ball — but the wicketkeeper may run out a batsman attempting a run off a No Ball.

Mankad: a bowler may run out a non-striker who has left the popping crease after the bowler starts the run-up, but only before entering the delivery stride.

## 10. Conduct, Discipline & Disputes

Dispute channel: In any dispute, only the captain and/or vice-captain may speak to the umpire — all communication must go through them. A team (captain, vice-captain, player, or supporter) that runs onto the field without the on-field umpires' consent loses 1 point.

On-field silence: No cheering, clapping, talking, or change of fielding position is allowed once the bowler's run-up starts, until the ball is completed. Umpires may call a No Ball if this happens.

Walking off: A team that walks away from a game over a disagreement with an umpire's decision is disqualified from the rest of the tournament. If it was their final league game, the playing XI is also barred from participating the following year.

Unsportsmanlike conduct (verbal abuse or taunting of an opponent or spectators, confirmed by the neutral on-field umpire and on-field organizer) escalates as follows:

1st offense — warning

2nd offense — 1-point deduction

3rd offense — disqualification from the tournament

## 13. Rain, Weather & Rescheduling

### 13.1 Determining Method

NPL uses the Duckworth–Lewis method to calculate revised targets for games affected by rain, low visibility, or wet ground conditions, via the calculator at

Second Innings Stopped: boltoncricket.co.uk/DLcalc.html

First Innings Stopped : https://www.omnicalculator.com/sports/duckworth-lewis

### 13.2 Overs Reduction

For every 5 minutes lost to rain or wet conditions, 1 over is deducted from each team's innings, with a minimum of 5 overs guaranteed per side.

Example: a 9:00 a.m. game that doesn't start until 10:30 a.m. is classified as rain-affected, and both teams split the points. Playing a 5-overs-a-side curtailed game requires a minimum of 50 minutes to complete 10 overs, to stay within the league schedule.

### 13.3 Resumption, Washouts & Definitions

The main umpire determines when it is safe to resume play after a rain delay.

A minimum of 5 overs played by both teams constitutes a game. A curtailed 5-over game has no powerplay or fielding restrictions, and only 1 over per bowler.

Washed-out game: a game washed out without a ball being bowled, including ground closures on the preceding Thursday or Friday to prevent field damage.

Rain-affected game: play starts and rain intervenes afterward — even a single ball bowled before rain means no reschedule, and regular rain rules apply.

Washed-out games are rescheduled to a reserved weekend before the playoffs, subject to ground and team availability, and can only be rescheduled once. If it rains again on that reserved weekend, regular rain rules apply and points are split.

Teams are asked to accommodate additional scheduling, including possible back-to-back games, given limited open slots; a team unable to accommodate a rescheduled slot will be declared a forfeit for that day.

Teams should come to the ground on a rain-affected day unless notified otherwise by the NPL Executive Team.

Rain-affected league games count as eligible games if the players were listed in the playing eleven; washed-out games do not count as eligible.

Playoff games have a reserve day for washouts. If a match on a reserve day cannot continue from where it left off, it restarts from the beginning; if washed out again, it moves to the next available playoff day.

## 14. Super Over (Tie-Breaker)

A Super Over decides ties in the Final, Semifinal, Quarterfinal, or Eliminator.

The team that batted second in the main match bats first in the Super Over.

The loss of 2 wickets ends the team's one-over Super Over innings.

The team scoring more runs wins.

If still tied, additional Super Overs are played until there is a winner.

A bowler who bowled in the first Super Over cannot bowl in a second one.

A batsman dismissed in the first Super Over cannot bat in a second one.

A batsman listed for the first Super Over who neither batted nor was dismissed remains eligible for a second Super Over.

## 15. Playing XI & Impact Player Rule

Before every match's toss, captains must list their Playing XI in the CricClubs scoring app, subject to umpire approval.

### 15.1 Impact Player

Each team may nominate one Impact Player before the toss, alongside the Playing XI. The player must already be part of the team's approved tournament squad.

The captain must declare the Impact Player's role as either Batting Impact Player or Bowling Impact Player.

Batting Impact Player: may bat, field, and keep wicket — cannot bowl.

Bowling Impact Player: may bowl (up to the standard 4-over maximum) and field — cannot bat.

The Impact Player may replace a teammate at the end of an over, at the fall of a wicket, at the innings break, or before either innings begins. The replaced player takes no further part in the match.

Each team gets only one Impact Player substitution per match, and must notify the on-field umpire and scorer before the substitution takes effect.

## 16. 12th Man (Substitute Fielder)

A 12th man may field (including as wicketkeeper) only for an injured player — tiredness does not qualify. The same substitute must be used for the rest of the match, and umpires must be notified before the 12th man takes the field.

A player must inform the umpire before leaving the field; the umpire tracks how many overs the player was off. The n/2 rule applies even for a single over off the field, and the player cannot immediately resume bowling upon returning.

## 17. Match Duration, Delay & Slow Over-Rate Penalties

### 17.1 Duration

Each T20 match is scheduled for 3 hours (180 minutes) total.

Each team is allotted 90 minutes to complete their innings.

A 5-minute drinks break may be taken after the 10th over of each innings, if a team requests it.

A maximum 10-minute innings break is allowed between innings; teams must be ready to resume play immediately after.

### 17.2 Delay Caused by One Team

If a match is delayed because one team fails to report on time, that team loses the toss and must field first.

For every 5 minutes of delay, 1 over is deducted from the delayed team's own batting innings only — the opposing team keeps its full allotment.

Example: if Team A reports 10 minutes late, Team B bats its full 20 overs while Team A is limited to a maximum of 18 overs while chasing.

### 17.3 Delay Caused by Both Teams

If both teams share responsibility for a delayed start, the match is reduced by 1 over per side for every 5 minutes of delay:

Powerplay overs and the maximum overs per bowler are adjusted according to the rain/reduced-overs table (Section 13.2).

### 17.4 Delay Beyond Either Team's Control

If the delay is caused by umpires, weather, ground conditions, or other circumstances outside either team's control, both teams receive equal over reductions, as determined by the Tournament Committee or Match Umpire.

### 17.5 Slow Over Rate

Each innings is expected to finish within the allotted 90 minutes, excluding scheduled drinks and innings breaks.

Batting-team delay: a penalty of 5 runs is deducted from the team's final score for every additional 5 minutes of delay it causes.

Fielding-team delay: once the 90 minutes elapse, a fielding side responsible for slow over rate is limited to just 4 fielders outside the 30-yard circle for the rest of the innings assuming the team playing with 11. Umpires may also impose further disciplinary action for repeated slow-over-rate offenses across the tournament.

## 18. Miscellaneous

COVID-19 withdrawal: if a team drops out of the tournament due to COVID-19 after the tournament has started, their opponents are declared the winner for all remaining league and playoff games against that team.
